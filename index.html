Loyola Weather Service

lws is an open source initiative being carried out by a group of researchers at Loyola University Chicago’s Computer Science Department lead by Senior Paul Stasiuk and faculty members George Thiruvathukal and Konstantin Läufer. The intended purpose of this project is to continue an ongoing effort in developing effective environmental monitoring tools that innovate in the field of computer science as well as contribute to a wider, scientific effort to analyze environmental variables and leverage the outputs for various initiatives. Our scope is focused on providing a higher awareness and implementation of sustainability. Through the use of these environmental variables and development, we plan on running pilot(s) in various building at the university and gather statistics on the current state. A higher level of sustainability can be realistically and easily achieved based on the outputs of the project. For example, the sensors can be placed in offices where they can collect environmental data and control the temperature based on if the offices are in use and report these changes. The savings in electricity(or gas, or water) could be exponentially staggering. This concept or framework could be easily repeated across multiple organizations, office complexes, and other initiatives where sustainability and cost savings are important. Scientifically, our research aims to contribute to concepts of RESTful frameworks, as well as a proper implementation of the “Internet of Things” concept(http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_of_Things). Additionally, we hope to add an element of “parallel processing” leveraging multiple sensors that work in tandem to efficiently utilize resources for processing and aggregation of data.
